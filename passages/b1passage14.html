Passage 14 · The Rise of Fast Fashion

👗 Fashion at a Cost

Fast fashion refers to the rapid production of inexpensive, trendy clothing. Brands like Zara, H&M, and Shein can design, manufacture, and deliver new styles to stores in just a few weeks. This model has revolutionized the clothing industry, making fashionable clothes affordable and accessible to millions of people around the world.

The benefits are clear: consumers can keep up with ever‑changing trends without spending much money. For young people on a budget, fast fashion offers a way to express their personal style. Moreover, the industry creates millions of jobs, particularly in developing countries where much of the production takes place.

However, the dark side of fast fashion is hard to ignore. The industry is one of the largest polluters globally. It consumes vast amounts of water and releases toxic chemicals into rivers. Synthetic fabrics like polyester shed microplastics that end up in the ocean. Additionally, the pressure to produce clothes quickly and cheaply often leads to poor working conditions and unfair wages in factories. Clothes are worn only a few times before being thrown away, creating enormous textile waste that clogs landfills.

In response, a growing movement of "slow fashion" encourages consumers to buy less, choose quality over quantity, and support brands that use sustainable practices. Thrifting and clothing swaps have also become popular. While change won't happen overnight, every thoughtful purchase can help shift the industry toward a more ethical and environmentally friendly future.
