Passage 5 · Why Sleep Matters

😴 Sleep: Your Body's Reset Button

Sleep is a fundamental human need, just like food and water. Every night, while we rest, our bodies and brains perform essential tasks that keep us healthy and functioning. Yet many people sacrifice sleep to work, study, or scroll through their phones, unaware of the serious consequences.

During sleep, the body repairs cells, strengthens the immune system, and releases important hormones. The brain, meanwhile, processes information from the day and forms memories – that's why a good night's sleep helps you learn better. Scientists call this "restorative" function.

Lack of sleep, on the other hand, can cause immediate problems: difficulty concentrating, irritability, and weakened defenses against illness. Over time, chronic sleep deprivation is linked to more serious conditions like heart disease, obesity, and depression. According to experts, adults need between seven and nine hours of quality sleep per night.

So how can we improve our sleep? Specialists recommend maintaining a consistent schedule, avoiding screens before bed, and creating a calm, dark environment. In our fast‑paced world, making sleep a priority is one of the smartest choices for long‑term well‑being.
