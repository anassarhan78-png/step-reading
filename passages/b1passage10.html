Passage 10 · The Importance of Bees

🐝 Small Creatures, Big Impact

Bees are small insects, but they play an enormous role in maintaining healthy ecosystems. There are over 20,000 species of bees worldwide, and they are best known for producing honey and wax. However, their most vital contribution is pollination.

As bees fly from flower to flower collecting nectar, they transfer pollen grains. This process, called pollination, allows plants to fertilize and produce fruits, seeds, and vegetables. In fact, about one‑third of the food we eat – including apples, almonds, coffee, and cucumbers – depends on pollinators like bees. Without them, many crops would fail, leading to food shortages and higher prices.

Sadly, bee populations are declining at an alarming rate. The main threats include pesticides used in farming, loss of wildflower meadows due to urban development, and diseases spread by parasites. Climate change also disrupts the timing of flower blooming, making it harder for bees to find food.

Fortunately, there are ways we can help. Planting bee‑friendly flowers in gardens, avoiding chemical sprays, and supporting local beekeepers all make a difference. Some cities are even creating "bee corridors" – networks of wildflower areas – to give bees safe routes. Protecting bees means protecting our own future. Every small action counts.
