Passage 20 · The Importance of Breakfast

🍳 Start Your Day Right

Breakfast is often described as "the most important meal of the day." After a long night of fasting, our bodies need fuel to function properly. Yet, in today's fast‑paced world, many people skip breakfast due to busy schedules or a desire to lose weight. Nutritionists, however, warn that this habit can have negative consequences.

Eating a balanced breakfast provides essential energy. The brain relies on glucose, which comes from the food we eat. Without it, you may feel sluggish, have difficulty concentrating, and experience mood swings. Studies have shown that children who eat breakfast perform better academically, with improved memory and attention spans.

Breakfast also kick‑starts your metabolism. When you eat in the morning, you signal your body that it doesn't need to conserve energy, helping you burn calories more efficiently throughout the day. People who regularly eat breakfast are less likely to overeat later, as they experience fewer hunger pangs. A healthy breakfast can include whole grains, protein (like eggs or yogurt), and fruits – a combination that provides sustained energy.

Of course, not all breakfasts are equal. A sugary pastry or a sugary cereal may give a quick energy spike, but it is often followed by a crash. Nutritionists recommend avoiding highly processed foods and instead choosing options rich in fiber and nutrients. In short, taking just 15 minutes to have a nutritious breakfast can set a positive tone for the entire day.
