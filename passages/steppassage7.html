Passage 7 (Advanced) · Data in Context: The Global Energy Transition

📊 Reading with Charts: Shifting Energy Landscapes

The global energy mix is undergoing a profound transformation, driven by climate policy, technological innovation, and shifting economics. While fossil fuels still dominate, renewables have grown at an unprecedented rate over the past decade. This transition is not uniform across regions, and the pace varies significantly. The charts below illustrate recent trends in electricity generation and capacity additions, providing a data-driven perspective on the energy transition.

Chart 1 shows the percentage share of global electricity generation by source in 2023. Coal and natural gas together still account for the majority, but wind and solar combined have surpassed hydropower. Chart 2 presents the growth of renewable electricity generation from 2010 to 2023, indexed to 2010 levels. The data reveals that solar and wind have experienced exponential growth, whereas hydropower has increased modestly. These trends underscore the accelerating shift toward low-carbon sources, though challenges remain in grid integration, storage, and infrastructure investment.
