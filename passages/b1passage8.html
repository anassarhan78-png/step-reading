Passage 8 · Clean Water: A Precious Resource

💧 Why Water Matters

Water is essential for all forms of life. It covers about 71% of Earth's surface, but only a small fraction – roughly 2.5% – is fresh water. Of that, most is trapped in glaciers and ice caps, leaving less than 1% of the world's water readily accessible for human use. This tiny share must sustain drinking, farming, sanitation, and industry for nearly eight billion people.

Access to clean water is a fundamental human right, yet millions still lack it. Contaminated water spreads diseases like cholera and typhoid, causing hundreds of thousands of deaths each year, especially among children. Beyond health, water scarcity affects food production and economic development. Farmers need reliable irrigation, and factories require water for manufacturing processes.

Several threats endanger our water supply. Pollution from industrial waste, agricultural chemicals, and plastic litter seeps into rivers and groundwater. Climate change alters rainfall patterns, leading to droughts in some regions and floods in others. Overuse of water for irrigation and household purposes depletes aquifers faster than they can naturally refill.

Solutions exist, but they require effort and cooperation. Individuals can conserve water by fixing leaks, taking shorter showers, and using efficient appliances. Governments must invest in water treatment plants and enforce anti‑pollution laws. International collaboration can help manage shared rivers and promote sustainable practices. In short, protecting clean water is a shared responsibility – and every drop counts.
